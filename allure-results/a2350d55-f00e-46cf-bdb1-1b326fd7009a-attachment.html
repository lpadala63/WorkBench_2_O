Variable Life Insurance Non-Guaranteed Elements

  For Variable Life Insurance, indicate the clients tolerance for non-guaranteed policy elements below.

Insurance products may have certain fees that can be adjusted at the discretion of the insurer after the contract is issued. These fees may include Mortality and Expense fees, face amount charges, rider fees, annual policy fees, or other product fees as described in the prospectus.

Insurers may reserve the right to change the guaranteed principal account interest rate for variable life insurance policies that offer a guaranteed principal account or similar option.

Insurers may reserve the right to change or limit the availability of investment options and/or the ability to transfer between investment options within the policy.

Insurers may provide guaranteed death benefit options for a variable policy, which could be for a period of years, to a certain age or lifetime. There may be required premiums, or limitations on investment allocations, withdrawals or transfers or loans, in order to maintain policy death benefit guarantees.

For specific details on policy guarantees and non-guaranteed elements, clients should consult the product prospectus and their financial professional.

Variable Universal Life insurance typically offers flexibility in terms of the premiums required to be paid, but in most cases the monthly expenses, including costs of insurance, must be paid or the policy will lapse.

Costs of insurance will typically increase over the life of the variable life insurance policy as the insureds age increases. If the policy account value decreases due to market losses in the separate accounts of a variable life policy, or withdrawals or policy loans, the premiums needed to keep the policy in force may increase.

Insurers may reserve the right to increase the costs of insurance to the guaranteed or maximum rates set out in the policy, which will result in higher premiums required to keep the policy in force.
